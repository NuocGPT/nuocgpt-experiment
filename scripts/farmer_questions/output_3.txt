3. Changes in River Flow: Deforestation in the upper parts of the Mekong basin can lead to changes in the river's flow patterns. Without trees to absorb and slowly release rainwater, more water can rush into the river at once, leading to faster and more erratic river flows. This can disrupt the balance between freshwater and saltwater in the delta, potentially leading to increased saltwater intrusion.

4. Increased Carbon Emissions: Deforestation contributes to climate change by releasing stored carbon dioxide into the atmosphere. Climate change can lead to sea-level rise and more intense storms, both of which can increase saltwater intrusion in the Mekong Delta.
